# AI Prompting in 2026

This directory contains learning materials, notes, templates, and experiments from the "AI Prompting in 2026" crash course in the Agent Factory curriculum. The course focuses on transforming how professionals interact with modern AI models—shifting from casual conversational prompting to rigorous context engineering and outcome verification.

## Core Philosophy

The central thesis of the course is:

> Prompting is not about clever wording. It is about controlling context.

Every advanced prompting technique serves one of two operational objectives:
1.  **Get the right context into the model.**
2.  **Keep the wrong context out of the model.**

By treating the AI as a smart but newly onboarded colleague rather than a simple search engine, users can harness its full reasoning capacity, web search tools, code execution environments, and multimodal features.

## Directory Contents

The files in this directory are organized as follows:

*   **notes.md**: The primary course study guide containing detailed summaries of the 13 core concepts, comparison rubrics, and the "Prompt Engineering Operating System."
*   **experiments.md**: A structured guide to the 12 hands-on prompting exercises recommended by the course. These experiments cover retrieval modes, neutral framing, single and multi-model critique, and sandboxed data analysis.
*   **prompt_templates.md**: Copy-pasteable Markdown templates for daily workflow automation, including smart briefs, strict search prompts, self-critique loops, and multi-model adjudication.
*   **reflections.md**: Analytical reflections on the cognitive shifts required in the AI era, including managing sycophancy, executing the 10-80-10 operating rhythm, and understanding the silent failure modes of AI tools.

## The 13 Concepts at a Glance

The course material is structured across four parts:

1.  **Part 1: How AI Knows Things**: Concept 1 (Novice vs. Power User), Concept 2 (Pretrained Knowledge boundaries), and Concept 3 (The Three Retrieval Modes: pretrained, web search, deep research).
2.  **Part 2: Talking to AI Well**: Concept 4 (Context Management), Concept 5 (Reasoning and "Think Hard" configurations), Concept 6 (Sycophancy neutralization), and Concept 7 (Brainstorm-Iterate Loop).
3.  **Part 3: Beyond Text**: Concept 8 (Multimodal data inputs), Concept 9 (One-Prompt interactive app building), and Concept 10 (Data Analysis via sandboxed code execution).
4.  **Part 4: Working Safely and Choosing Tools**: Concept 11 (Desktop permissions and security), Concept 12 (Resource profiling and model selection), and Concept 13 (Multi-model cross-checking/models checking models).

Refer to the respective markdown files to study or apply these concepts.
