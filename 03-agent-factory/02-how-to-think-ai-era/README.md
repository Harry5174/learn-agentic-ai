# How to Think in the AI Era

This directory contains learning materials, study notes, and actionable checklists from the "How to Think in the AI Era" crash course. The course teaches the core thinking disciplines and cognitive habits that prevent professionals from delegating their critical judgment to AI systems.

## The Core Rule

The central axiom of this module is:

> The deliverable is never the answer. The deliverable is the documented evidence of thinking.

When using AI, the output generated by the model is cheap and immediate. The load-bearing element of any task is the human reasoning, verification, and critical overrides that shape the final result.

## Module Structure

This directory is organized as follows:

*   **notes.md**: Detailed study notes covering the six cognitive disciplines, the six corresponding AI failure modes they address, and the theoretical underpinnings of each method.
*   **thinking_checklists.md**: Actionable, copy-pasteable checklists and templates for executing each discipline in daily professional workflows.

## The Six Disciplines

The course is structured across three parts, matching six disciplines to six specific AI failure modes:

### Part 1: Foundations (The Posture)
1.  **The Prediction Lock**: Write down your predicted answer before consulting AI to neutralize the anchoring effect and preserve independent judgment.
2.  **The Reasoning Receipt**: Document every decision made during a session (ACCEPT, REJECT, MODIFY, SURFACED, MISSED) with explicit justifications to prevent passive acceptance.

### Part 2: Detection (Catching What AI Misses)
3.  **The Error Taxonomy**: Scan AI output systematically for six specific categories of errors (Factual Error, Logical Gap, False Confidence, Missing Context, Fabricated Source, Stale Fact) rather than reading for general flow.
4.  **Thinking in Systems**: Map second-order and third-order consequences using Cascade Maps to identify feedback loops that might cause a decision to backfire.

### Part 3: Origination (Doing What AI Cannot)
5.  **First Principles**: Identify "Named Thresholds" (specific conditions or numbers) where consensus advice or common rules of thumb fail to hold.
6.  **Working WITH AI**: Execute the Three-Path Comparison (Solo vs. AI-Only vs. Collaborative) to ensure human overrides are documented and clear.
